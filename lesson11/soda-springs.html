What is a Soda spring?

Soda Springs is a rare, accessible, naturally carbonated groundwater source. Flowing though calcium carbonate rock, the spring water acquires a carbonated fizz that was long thought to give the water medicinal benefits. In some places, the original springs have been restored for public use.

Lying at 4,400 feet, the scenic valley has had an interesting history. The tribes inhabited the area for centuries before the arrival of foreign settlers during the 19th-century California gold rush. In addition to gold mining, the area also became known for cattle ranching and even summer resorts. The original owners acquired the land in 1930, leasing it to ranchers for livestock grazing after a plan to build hydroelectric containers.
