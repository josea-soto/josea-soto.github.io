Hick's Law

Apple

https://www.apple.com/iphone/

By simplifying the number of choices that a user faces in apple's website, they encourage decision making. We can see there is almost no other choice but scrolling to continue exploring iphone's features. Hick's law, or the Hickâ€“Hyman law, describes the time it takes for a person to make a decision as a result of the possible choices: increasing the number of choices will increase the decision time logarithmically.

Fitt's Law

Codecademy

https://www.codecademy.com/

Menu options are located at the top of the page, which makes it simple for the user to reach them. Additionally, log-in or sign-up buttons and text boxes are grouped together, facilitating their selection one after the other. Grouping is important in communicating the functionality. Fitt's law states that selecting a UI element can be faster if the size is increased or the distance decreased.

Rule of Thirds

Dribbble

https://dribbble.com/

Dribble's landing page shows an image which is approximately one-third of the total horizontal space and two-thirds of the total vertical space. The guideline proposes that an image should be imagined as sectionided into nine equal parts by two equally spaced horizontal lines and two equally spaced vertical lines, and that important compositional elements should be placed along these lines or their intersections. Proponents of the technique claim that aligning a subject with these points creates more tension, energy and interest in the composition than simply centering the subject.
